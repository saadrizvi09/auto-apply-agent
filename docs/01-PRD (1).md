# Product Requirements Document (PRD)
### Project: AutoApply — Free Job-Application Emailer
**Version:** 1.1 · **Status:** Final · **Owner:** You · **Last updated:** 2026-06-18

---

## 1. Summary

AutoApply is a locally-run tool that discovers **live remote engineering openings at
small, reachable startups** by scraping LinkedIn's public guest jobs endpoint (no
login) across India, the US and the EU, finds an HR contact for each via Hunter.io,
sends a **personalized application email with a PDF CV attached**, and **monitors the
inbox** to track the status of every application. It runs entirely on free service
tiers. US/EU rows get a "remote-from-India" cost/quality pitch; India rows use the
standard application.

It is operated through a simple browser dashboard with five stage buttons:
**Find Jobs → Find Contacts → Draft Emails → Review & Send → Scan Replies.**

## 2. Problem

Applying to many companies for fresher SDE roles is slow and repetitive: finding who's
hiring, finding a contact, writing a tailored email, sending, and then losing track of
who replied. Doing it manually caps the volume; doing it naively (mass-blasting generic
mail to scraped addresses) gets the sender's email account blocked and converts poorly.

## 3. Goals

| # | Goal |
|---|---|
| G1 | Surface real, current remote openings at small reachable startups matching the user's filters with minimal manual searching |
| G2 | Send genuinely personalized emails at the **highest sustainable volume** without the sender's account being blocked |
| G3 | Keep bounce rate low (verified recipients) to protect sender reputation |
| G4 | Give a single dashboard showing the live status of every application |
| G5 | Cost the user ₹0 in software/services |

## 4. Non-Goals

- Not a mass-spam blaster. Volume is bounded by deliverability safety, not ambition.
- Not a guaranteed-delivery or guaranteed-response system.
- Not a replacement for referrals/LinkedIn — it is a **supporting channel**.
- No paid hosting, no paid email infrastructure, no multi-account rotation.
- Not a logged-in LinkedIn scraper: discovery reads only LinkedIn's **public guest
  jobs endpoint** (no account, no auth). This still carries IP-ban risk and is not
  officially sanctioned (see §9).

## 5. Target user & persona

**Primary user:** A job seeker (the operator) targeting SDE-1 / internship roles in
Delhi NCR or remote, comfortable running a local app and clicking through stages.
Single-user product. No multi-tenancy.

## 6. User stories

- As the user, I can **enter search filters** (role, location, remote, keywords) and get a list of live openings.
- As the user, I can **auto-find a contact email or apply link** for each opening, with a verification status.
- As the user, I can **generate a tailored draft** per opening that references the real role and company.
- As the user, I can **review every draft and approve only the ones I want** before anything sends.
- As the user, I can **send approved emails** with my CV attached, throttled to stay safe.
- As the user, I can **see each application's status** (sent, replied-interview, rejection, bounced, no-reply) on one dashboard.
- As the user, the tool **checks my inbox automatically** and updates statuses without me re-running it.

## 7. Scope

### In scope (v1)
- Discovery via LinkedIn's public guest jobs endpoint, across default roles
  (AI Agents Engineer, LLM Engineer, Backend Engineer) and geos (India, US, Germany),
  remote-only. Filters drop internships, over-senior titles, big-company/IT-services/
  staffing firms, salaries below a Min LPA threshold (when shown), and companies over a
  Max team size — biasing toward small reachable startups. (The legacy Google Custom
  Search over ATS domains is kept only as a disabled fallback; its CSE key 403s.)
- Contact resolution: HR emails resolved from the company name via Hunter.io
  domain-search, with Hunter-confidence verification.
- LLM-tailored drafting (standard prompt for India rows, remote-from-India pitch for
  US/EU rows) and reply classification (Groq).
- Gmail send + read via OAuth.
- SQLite-backed status tracking + browser dashboard with 5 buttons.
- Anti-ban sending controls (cap, ramp, delay, verify, auto-pause).
- Convenience inputs: bulk CSV/Excel import, a "Paste & apply" dump box, and a résumé
  autofill that reads `cv.pdf` to fill blank `profile.json` fields.

### Out of scope (v1)
- Automated follow-up sequences (manual for now).
- Multiple sender accounts / rotation.
- CRM integrations, analytics beyond the status table.
- Resume generation/optimization.

## 8. Success metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Bounce rate | < 3% (auto-pause if exceeded) |
| Sender account status | Not blocked/throttled over a 30-day run |
| Personalization | 100% of sent emails reference the specific role + a company-specific line |
| Reply-tracking accuracy | ≥ 90% of human replies classified correctly |
| Operating cost | ₹0 |

## 9. Constraints & assumptions

- **Sender:** an **aged** personal Gmail with good standing (preferred over a new account).
- **Realistic volume:** configurable daily cap, ramped; safe sustainable range ≈ 40–50/day for a reputable aged Gmail. The 500/day Gmail ceiling is a hard limit, not a target. Behavioral blocks can occur well below it if bounces/spam signals appear.
- Free-tier limits apply (see Build Runbook §Cheat-sheet): Groq ~1,000 req/day,
  Hunter **50 lookups/month**. Each Find Jobs run caps Hunter lookups so one click
  can't exhaust the monthly quota; when the quota is spent, HR-email resolution pauses
  until the monthly reset.
- An **optional backup Groq key** (`GROQ_API_KEY_BACKUP`) is used automatically on a
  429 rate-limit from the primary key.
- **LinkedIn ban risk:** discovery scrapes LinkedIn's public guest endpoint without an
  account. It is not officially sanctioned, may break if LinkedIn changes the endpoint,
  and can get the operator's IP temporarily rate-limited/blocked. Runs are kept small
  (3 roles × 3 geos, capped cards per query) to stay gentle.
- Runs locally (Windows/Mac/Linux). **Not** deployed to Vercel — serverless cron (once/day), short function timeouts, and ephemeral storage make it unsuitable.

## 10. Key risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Sender account blocked | Daily cap + ramp, randomized delays, verify-before-send, bounce auto-pause, full personalization, PDF attached (no links) |
| High bounce rate from guessed emails | Hunter verification gate; prefer apply-links from the posting itself |
| Channel is low-yield for SDE roles | Reframed around real openings; positioned as a supporting channel in docs/UI |
| Free-tier exhaustion (Hunter 50/mo) | Fall back to pattern + SMTP-check; keep cap modest |
| OAuth token leakage | Token stored locally only; never deployed; `.env` and `token.json` git-ignored |

## 11. Expectation-setting (must surface in UI)

The dashboard should display a one-line banner: *"Supporting channel. Run alongside
referrals and LinkedIn. Volume is capped for deliverability safety, and discovery is
bounded by the Hunter 50-lookups/month free tier."*

## 12. Addendum — Google-Forms auto-apply (referral digests)

Added after v1. Many job channels forward "Referral Alert" digest emails: a numbered
list of roles, each ending in **either a Google Form link or an email-to-apply**.

- **Ingest**: paste the digest (or scan Gmail). It is split into structured jobs and
  routed — Google-Form rows become *form applications*; email-to-apply rows feed the
  existing draft→send pipeline.
- **Pre-fill**: for each form job the tool reads the form's questions (with their entry
  IDs), maps them to a one-time **applicant profile** (`profile.json`), answers
  open-ended questions via Groq, and builds a **pre-filled link**
  (`viewform?usp=pp_url&entry.X=…`). Under `DRY_RUN=true` answers use a deterministic
  stub so a preview costs no Groq quota.
- **Open-review-submit in your own Chrome**: the operator opens the pre-filled link in
  their own logged-in **real Chrome** (`channel="chrome"`; the bundled Chromium is
  blocked by Google at sign-in), reviews it, **attaches the CV manually** (Google blocks
  programmatic file upload + submit), then submits. Nothing is submitted automatically.
- **Honest limits**: file-upload (CV) questions and missing profile fields are *flagged
  for the operator*, never fabricated. One-application-per-(form-URL + role). Form cards
  can be **archived** (kept in history) or **permanently deleted**.

This keeps the product's core discipline — local-only, operator-approved, nothing acts
without explicit confirmation — while removing the manual drudgery of repetitive forms.
